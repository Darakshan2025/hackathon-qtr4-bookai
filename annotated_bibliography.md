# Annotated Bibliography and Verified Source List

## Annotated Bibliography

*   **"Agentic AI for Software: thoughts from Software Engineering community"** by Abhik Roychoudhury (arXiv, 2025)
    *   **Annotation:** This paper explores the role of AI agents in various software engineering tasks, including code generation, testing, program repair, architecture exploration, and requirements understanding. It envisions AI agents as members of a development team, emphasizing the importance of inferring developer intent and the need for AI-based verification and validation in future agentic software workflows.
    *   **DOI:** [10.48550/arXiv.2508.17343](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2508.17343)

*   **"Agentic AI Frameworks: Architectures, Protocols, and Design Challenges"** by Hana Derouiche, Zaki Brahmi, and Haithem Mazeni (arXiv, 2025, and copyrighted by IEEE)
    *   **Annotation:** This article provides a systematic review and comparative analysis of prominent Agentic AI frameworks such as CrewAI, LangGraph, and AutoGen. It evaluates their architectural principles, communication mechanisms, memory management, and safety protocols, also addressing key limitations and future research directions in the field.
    *   **DOI:** [10.48550/arXiv.2508.10146](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2508.10146)

*   **"AI Agentic Programming: A Survey of Techniques, Challenges, and Opportunities"** (arXiv)
    *   **Annotation:** This survey offers a comprehensive overview of the emerging AI agentic programming paradigm. It introduces a taxonomy of agent behaviors and system architectures, examining techniques related to planning, context management, tool integration, execution monitoring, and benchmarking datasets, with a focus on LLM-driven agentic systems for software development.
    *   **DOI:** [10.48550/arXiv.2508.11126](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2508.11126)

*   **"Agentic Software Engineering: Foundational Pillars and a Research Roadmap"** (arXiv, 2025)
    *   **Annotation:** This paper introduces "Agentic Software Engineering (SE 3.0)," where intelligent agents are designed to achieve complex, goal-oriented software engineering objectives. It discusses the challenges posed by the "speed vs. trust" gap in agent-generated code and outlines a research roadmap for structured agentic software engineering.
    *   **DOI:** [10.48550/arXiv.2509.06216](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2509.06216)

*   **"Advanced Architectures Integrated with Agentic AI for Next-Generation Wireless Networks"** by Kapal Dev, Sunder Ali Khowaja, Kapal Singh, Engin Zeydan, and Merouane Debbah (ResearchGate, 2025)
    *   **Annotation:** This article investigates cutting-edge technologies and architectural innovations for simplifying network operations in next-generation wireless networks. This includes exploring advanced techniques for constrained AI operations and the design of AI agents for real-time learning and resource optimization.
    *   **DOI:** [10.48550/arXiv.2502.01089](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2502.01089)
